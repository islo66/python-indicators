Thus, pivot to improve the current solution. The entering variable is $y_1$ and the departing variable is $s_1$.\end{flushleft}\begin{flushleft}Perform elementary row operations until the pivot element is 1 and all other elements in the entering column are 0.\end{flushleft}\begin{equation*}\begin{bmatrix}\begin{array}{cccc|c}y_1 &y_2 &s_1 &s_2 &b \\ \hline1 & 1/2 & 1/2 & 0 & 3/2 \\0 & 1/2 & -1/2 & 1 & 1/2 \\ \hline0 & -1 & 3 & 0 & 9 \\\end{array}\end{bmatrix}\begin{array}{c}\\y_1 \\s_2 \\\\\end{array}\end{equation*}\begin{flushleft}There are negative elements in the bottom row, so the current solution is not optimal. Thus, pivot to improve the current solution. The entering variable is $y_2$ and the departing variable is $s_2$.\end{flushleft}\begin{flushleft}Perform elementary row operations until the pivot element is 1 and all other elements in the entering column are 0.\end{flushleft}\begin{equation*}\begin{bmatrix}\begin{array}{cccc|c}y_1 &y_2 &s_1 &s_2 &b \\ \hline1 & 0 & 1 & -1 & 1 \\0 & 1 & -1 & 2 & 1 \\ \hline0 & 0 & 2 & 2 & 10 \\\end{array}\end{bmatrix}\begin{array}{c}\\y_1 \\y_2 \\\\\end{array}\end{equation*}\begin{flushleft}There are no negative elements in the bottom row, so we know the solution is optimal. Thus, the solution is: \end{flushleft}\begin{equation*}s_1 = 0, s_2 = 0, x_1 = 2, x_2 = 2, y_1 = 1, y_2 = 1, z = 10\end{equation*}\end{document}
